# Soil Intelligence — Report Family Recipe Matrix

**Family code:** `RF-SOI` · **Target:** 24 reports · **Persona / who-gets-fired:** the **Agronomist** (and the regional farm manager / regen-ag program lead who signs off on fertility, amendments, and carbon).

## Persona & the fear it answers

The agronomist owns the invisible half of the P&L: **fertility, amendments, and carbon**. They are the person who gets fired when a $400k fertilizer program goes onto ground that couldn't hold it, when a salinity front or compaction pan quietly caps yield below the Dole/Del Monte contract quota, or when a Carbon-MRV auditor rejects a season of soil-carbon claims and torpedoes a buyer's regenerative-sourcing commitment to Walmart. This family is the executive answer to *"Am I spending amendment dollars on the right acres, is the ground physically capable of hitting the yield I promised the CEO, and can I defend every soil-health and carbon number in an audit?"* It is not a soil map — it is a spend-defense and quota-defense instrument. Every recipe is honest about tier: **T1 deterministic** (terrain, drainage, indices), **T2 relative/screening** (moisture, salinity, nutrient stress ranking), **T3 model-inferred estimate** (SOC stock, texture, CEC, P/K) — never a lab-grade or regulatory claim; ground-truth soil sampling is always positioned as the calibration partner, not the competitor.
