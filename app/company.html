MISSION

The most expensive change on a farm is the one nobody sees in time.

Every year, up to 40% of the world's crops are lost to pests and disease — much of it detectable early, as stress, weeks before it's obvious from the cab. Farming runs on roughly 70% of the planet's freshwater withdrawals, so a water problem missed for a fortnight is money gone. Report.Farm exists to close that window: to give the people who own crops, land, and budgets a continuous, evidence-grade view of what changed and what it's worth, in time to act.

We do it standards-first — running on open Sentinel-2, Sentinel-1 and Landsat Earth observation for a low-cost, high-frequency baseline, and escalating to commercial imagery only when a finding warrants it. The result a grower gets is simple: fewer surprises, earlier action, and a defensible record of what was known and when.

ORIGIN

Built by the people who needed it in the field.

Report.Farm was founded in 2024 by a group of operators with field experience across agronomy, geospatial intelligence, and Earth-observation programs. We weren't trying to start a satellite company. We were trying to solve a problem we kept hitting: by the time crop stress, a water problem, or a change on the land reached someone's screen, it had already cost weeks, yield, and margin.

The tools available were either pixel shops — selling raw imagery with no workflow — or dashboards that never explained what a change meant or what it was worth. Nothing tied space-based observation to a real decision to the ground in a way a farm manager, a landowner, or a sourcing team could actually act on and defend.

So our founders built the platform they wanted but didn't have. A live farm digital twin, standards-first Earth observation, evidence-grade reports, role-based access, and an intelligence layer that explains what changed, why, how confident it is, and what it will cost. Every choice in the product is shaped by one bias: it has to hold up in the field, and it has to hold up when a lender or buyer asks what you knew.

We are a small, founder-led team. We don't have a billboard at the airport. We have customers running pilots, a security and compliance program we are executing against, and a deep conviction that the people who grow the world's food deserve software built by people who understand the field.

VALUES

Field-built operating principles.

⊕

Outcome-first

Every feature is judged against one question: does it move a grower's number in the field? If not, it doesn't ship.

⌬

Evidence-grade

Every finding is scored, reproducible, and exportable in formats that hold up in lender, insurer and buyer reviews.

⚞

Standards-first

Open geospatial and farm standards before vendor lock-in — STAC, OGC, COG, ISOBUS/ISOXML — so your data stays yours and portable.

◔

Field-built

The product is shaped by people who have walked fields, worked satellite data, and advised growers. Software supports the operator — it doesn't replace judgement.

◉

Transparency

If we don't know, we say so. If a compliance milestone is underway but not yet audited, we say "underway, not yet audited" — every time.

⊞

Your data, isolated

Multi-tenant with strict per-tenant isolation. Your fields, your data, your audit trail — never blended with anyone else's.
