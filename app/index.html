Frequently asked

The questions we hear most.

How is Report.Farm priced?
Pricing scales with the land under monitoring and the cadence you need. Routine monitoring runs on free open Earth observation (Sentinel-2, Landsat), so baseline coverage is inexpensive; commercial imagery and tasking are billed only when a farm profile or alert threshold calls for it. Start with a single field or supplier and grow to a full portfolio — contact us for a tailored plan.
How do you see change before it's obvious?
We fuse free satellite imagery, SAR radar that sees through cloud and night, weather and optional field sensors into a live digital twin of each farm, then run vegetation indices and change detection against each field's own baseline. Each finding arrives with a confidence level and dated satellite evidence, is verified before it becomes an action, and sharpens as outcomes are fed back. We sell the result — farm intelligence you can act on — not the underlying method.
What is your compliance posture?
Tenant isolation, per-mutation audit logging and role-based access are enforced at the schema level today. A SOC 2 program and GDPR/CCPA alignment are underway; the platform is built against these controls, with independent audit ahead of general availability. Not yet independently certified.
How is Report.Farm deployed?
Default deployment is multi-tenant cloud. The heavy geospatial and ML work is delegated to an independent geospatial core, so the farm layer stays lightweight; co-located and self-hosted options are available for teams that need data to stay on their own infrastructure.
Where does our data live?
Tenants choose a primary data residency region at provisioning time. Every business table carries a tenant_id enforced at the schema level, with a check that blocks any new table from shipping without it — so no buyer ever sees another's suppliers.
How do we start?
Request a farm plan from the contact page. We scope your fields or supplier network, confirm satellite coverage over your area of interest, and stand up a tenant with a baseline — from a single field to an entire portfolio, with your first report in short order.
